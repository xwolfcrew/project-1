When Brooklyn Community Foundation launched in 2009, Brooklyn nonprofits received just 2% of all NYC foundation funding. Since then, Brooklyn Community Foundation has moved over $100 million to Brooklyn nonprofits, shown up for the borough in times of need, and deployed new models for participatory philanthropy and community engagement. But Brooklyn deserves more.

After nearly 15 years, the foundation understood that to truly make a difference in the status quo, they would need to make a dramatic shift. Mother New York and Mother Design developed a strategy to distance the organization from notions of traditional philanthropy, seen largely today as elitist, dysfunctional, and detached.

Brooklyn Community Foundation should be reborn not only in look and feel, we posited, but in name: Brooklyn Org would be the champion for Brooklyn, the new model for community philanthropy, and the destination for all who care.
